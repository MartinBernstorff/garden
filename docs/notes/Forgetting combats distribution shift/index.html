Forgetting combats distribution shift

By forgetting outdated information.

This implies that we don’t want to memorise information that isn’t durable.

Q. Why might we want to avoid memorising information that changes (eg. treatment guidelines)? A. Increased risk of future errors

Q. When might we memorise information in spite of it changing over time? A. If 1) We need it rapidly or 2) We’ll spend more than 5 minutes looking it up before it changes
